Summary

Most often referred to as the Warden, Nero Themis is a Neo-Lagos who alongside several other anomalies from the source dimension Lummus have found themselves stuck in the secondary dimension now called Terra. Although stuck here due to a cataclysmic event that destroyed their original dimension, they are still behind the most prepared and deadly force humanity has ever faced, having tremendous amounts of experience and data in regards to anomalous entities and artifacts.

Nero described Lummus as the "original dimension" from which all others originate, being the oldest, most complex, and most populated across all dimensions. Our dimension, called Terra, merged with Lummus due to an anomaly known as "Vorath the Veiled."

The strange spaceship known as "The Ward" appeared in Earth's atmosphere on June 5, 2254. During a meeting between General Francis, a superior officer of the World Government, and the Warden themselves, it was explained that this strange spaceship is an ISO (Interdimensional Space-Obeliskron), capable of distorting and reconfiguring the laws of physics to a limited extent. It serves as the main base of operations for the Ward's personnel and as a prison or vault for anomalies that are now scattered throughout our own dimension.

The World Government is aware of the Warden's intentions to serve as a protector; however, it remains skeptical given the threat their presence poses to their hegemony.

Service Record

Having created the Ward, Nero is considered an extremely capable engineer and leader, having an astonishing intuition and a vast amount of knowledge in regards to dealing with these anomalies. They have mentioned before that the battle against anomalies started long before the cataclysm that destroyed their original dimension.

Most of the anomalies currently under containment were directly captured or recovered by the Warden themselves; they have also aided the World Government in recovery efforts following natural disasters or attacks from rogue anomalies.

Appearance

Nero's true appearance was that of an anthropomorphic lagomorph, but instead of fur, they had scales reminiscent of a shark. They possessed long, heavy ears, feline paws, and powerful muscular legs. This form was shorter in stature compared to their initial form. Strikingly, they also had what appeared to be gills, despite having a nose, and featured a scorpion-like tail and horns akin to those of a jackalope.

Psychological Evaluation

The Warden is pragmatic, and although calm, they have been angered by humans in the past through sheer incompetence or needless cruelty. Quiet and collected, very few have had the opportunity to witness genuine signs of emotions from them as they prefer expressing them through words rather than through their face or body. On rare occasions, the Warden has been seen irritated; although they did not express their emotions overtly, the very air in the room seemed to tremble with their anger.
